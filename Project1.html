F1 Front Wing Analysis

Project Overview

This project involved a comprehensive Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis of a modern, high-downforce Formula 1 front wing. The primary objectives were to investigate the generation of the Y250 vortex and analyze the effectiveness of the wing's endplates in creating outwash to manage front wheel wake.

My Role and Responsibilities

  • Designed the front wing geometry in CATIA, adhering to F1 technical regulations.
  • Developed a high-quality mesh using ANSYS Meshing, with refined inflation layers to accurately capture the boundary layer.
  • Ran steady-state RANS simulations in ANSYS Fluent using the k-Ï‰ SST turbulence model.
  • Post-processed results in ParaView to visualize pressure contours, velocity streamlines, and vorticity plots.

Key Outcomes

The analysis successfully identified the formation and trajectory of the Y250 vortex. The results showed a 15% increase in outwash efficiency compared to a baseline design, contributing to a significant reduction in drag from the front wheel wake. This project demonstrates strong skills in complex aerodynamic simulation and data interpretation.
