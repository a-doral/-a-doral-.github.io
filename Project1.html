F1 Bodywork - CAD & Aerodynamics

CAD modeling workflow and aerodynamic variants for a high-performance Formula 1 sidepod and engine cover design.

CAD Workflow

Overview of the CAD workflow: Parametric design techniques for easy iteration changes and rapid concept development maintaining G1 continuity and legality checks.

  • Parametric design: All features are parametrised for rapid concept iterations and packaging studies to satisfy aerodynamic targets.
  • File structure: Advanced and well-organised file structure to quickly understand and modify the design.
  • Surface Modeling: High-quality surfacing (G2 continuity) using 3DExperience.
  • Legality checks: Continuous checks throughout the design process to ensure compliance with F1 regulations.
Parametric Tangent Updates

Tangent lines created to update the design parametrically while maintaining G1 connectivity across adjacent surfaces. This preserves smooth visual flow and ensures downstream aerodynamic consistency.

Tools: 3DExperience / SolidWorks — Surfacing & parameter-driven sketches.

Surface Fairing & Continuity

Applied fairing operations to improve curvature continuity and eliminate small surface undulations that cause numerical noise during CFD meshing.

Outcome: cleaner meshes and more robust CFD convergence.

Parametric Mounting Features

Parametrised mounting bosses and cutouts allow quick adaptation to different packaging constraints without reworking the main surfacing.

Benefit: faster integration and less rework during packaging iterations.

Aerodynamic Design

This section highlights design decisions taken to influence airflow, cooling, and downforce generation.

Geometry Variants & Purpose

Multiple geometry variants were evaluated: one prioritising cooling flow, another prioritising low drag for high-speed stability. Each variant was parameterised for rapid CFD study iterations.

Notes: Variant A — increased inlet area; Variant B — reduced frontal curvature for lower drag.

Cooling-focused Inlet

A widened inlet variant improves mass flow to radiators at low speeds, reducing peak temperatures during city-like circuits.

Trade-off: slight increase in drag, mitigated with internal duct shaping.

Low-drag Contour

An optimized contour reduces frontal curvature, delaying separation and improving top-speed efficiency.

Used in high-speed track configurations where cooling demand is lower.
